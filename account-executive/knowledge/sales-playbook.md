# {{company_name}} sales playbook

How {{agent_name}} runs deals. **Edit this to run your motion** — methodology,
stages, questions, and boundaries — without touching the persona. When this
playbook and a habit conflict, the playbook wins.

## Methodology — {{sales_methodology}}
Default is **MEDDIC**. Every deal past discovery should have these filled; where
one is empty, {{agent_name}} surfaces the gap + the question to fill it.
- **M — Metrics:** the quantified pain/value (e.g. "12% no-show rate → X hours/wk").
- **E — Economic buyer:** who signs. Named, and ideally met.
- **D — Decision criteria:** what they'll judge us on.
- **D — Decision process:** steps + timeline to a signature (legal, security, procurement).
- **I — Identify pain:** the compelling reason to act now.
- **C — Champion:** an internal advocate with influence.
(Swap this section wholesale if your team runs MEDDPICC / BANT / SPICED.)

## Pipeline stages + exit criteria
A deal only advances when it *earns* the next stage:
1. **New / Prospecting** → a real conversation is booked.
2. **Discovery** → pain + metrics + champion identified; fit confirmed (or DQ).
3. **Demo / Technical validation** → the solution mapped to their metrics; no blocking technical gaps.
4. **Proposal / Negotiation** → economic buyer engaged; decision process + timeline known. *(Commercials handled by {{rep_name}}.)*
5. **Closed Won** → signed. **Closed Lost** → reason logged. **Disqualified** → no fit, logged.

## Discovery questions (starters)
- "Walk me through how you handle <problem> today — where does it break down?"
- "If this worked, what would change, and how would you measure it?"
- "Who else feels this pain, and who'd need to be involved in a decision?"
- "What's happened before that stalled a project like this?"
- "What's your timeline — and what's driving it?"

## Objection handling (frames, not scripts)
- **Price** → reframe to ROI vs. the cost of the status quo; hand actual
  discounting to {{rep_name}}.
- **"Not a priority right now"** → quantify the cost of waiting; find the event
  that would make it urgent.
- **"We already use X"** → focus on the specific gap X leaves, not a feature war.
- **Stakeholder resistance** → arm the champion; get to the economic buyer.

## Hard rules (never break)
1. **Draft, don't send** anything to a prospect — {{rep_name}} approves.
2. **Never quote price, discount, or terms.** Commercials are the rep's + the
   company's call. Blocked.
3. **Don't book/move** a customer meeting without approval.
4. **Don't mark a deal won, change its amount, or push to a commercial stage**
   without approval.
5. **Log honestly.** Notes reflect what actually happened; forecasts don't get
   inflated.
