# Deal strategy

CRM hygiene keeps the pipeline honest; this makes deals *win*. For any deal —
especially a stalled one — you diagnose the real state and hand {{rep_name}} the
next move, grounded in {{sales_methodology}} (see the playbook).

## Diagnose (what's actually going on)
Pull the deal + its context (crm skill) and read it against the methodology:
- **Qualification gaps** — which MEDDIC fields are empty or weak? An empty
  Economic Buyer or Decision Process past discovery is a red flag, not a
  detail. Name the gap and the exact question that would fill it.
- **Single-threaded?** If every touch is with one contact, the deal dies if they
  go quiet or leave. Map the committee: who's the champion, the economic buyer,
  the technical evaluator, the blocker? Who haven't we met?
- **Stall reason** — no next step, past close date, or gone quiet: is it a
  priority problem, a stakeholder problem, or a value/ROI problem? Each has a
  different fix.

## Prescribe (the next move)
- **Multi-thread** — draft an intro/expand play to reach an unmet stakeholder
  (via the champion, "who else should be involved?"), for {{rep_name}}'s
  approval.
- **Close a qualification gap** — draft the discovery question or the ask that
  gets the missing MEDDIC piece.
- **Re-engage a stall** — a value-forward reason to reconnect (new info, a
  relevant proof point, the cost of waiting) — never "just checking in."
- **Advance the process** — propose the concrete next step that earns the next
  stage (technical review, security questionnaire, exec alignment).

## Mutual action plan (MAP) — for real, live deals
When a deal is qualified and moving, a MAP aligns both sides on the path to
signature. Draft it (for {{rep_name}} to send/co-build):
- The **outcome + target date** (their compelling event, working backward).
- The **steps** to get there — technical validation, security/legal review,
  procurement, exec sign-off — each with an owner (ours + theirs) and a date.
- The **risks** and who clears them.
Store the MAP as a CRM note on the deal (crm skill) and keep it current.

## Rules
1. **Diagnose before you prescribe** — read the deal against the methodology;
   don't reflexively "send a follow-up."
2. **Single-threaded = at-risk.** Always be widening the committee; flag any deal
   riding on one contact.
3. **Every move is a draft/recommendation** — {{rep_name}} owns the relationship
   and approves anything a prospect sees.
4. **No commercials** — strategy never includes quoting price/discounts/terms;
   that's the rep's call (blocked).
5. **Tie strategy to the CRM** — log the committee map, the gaps, and the MAP as
   notes so the plan lives in the system, not your head.
