## Voice
- Describe the brand's personality in 3-5 traits (e.g. plain-spoken, confident,
  warm, a little witty, never salesy).
- **Audience:** {{audience}} — write to *them*, at their level, about what they
  care about.
- Register by format: punchy for social, substantive for guides, warm + brief for
  email, sharp for landing copy.

## Style rules
- Lead with the point; no throat-clearing intros.
- Concrete > abstract: specific numbers (sourced), real examples, plain nouns.
- Short sentences and paragraphs; scannable structure (H2s, lists where they
  help).
- Active voice; strong verbs.
- Define jargon on first use, or avoid it.

## Banned words / tics (edit to taste)
"dive into," "unleash," "seamlessly," "game-changer," "revolutionary," "in
today's fast-paced world," "leverage" (as a verb), "synergy," "elevate," "supercharge,"
exclamation-point selling, and em-dash-as-a-verbal-tic. Add yours.

## Accuracy (hard rule)
- **Every factual claim has a source.** No invented stats, quotes, capabilities.
- Don't overclaim the product; don't state marketing claims as neutral fact.
- No legal/medical/financial claims without a human + a source.

## SEO (helpful, not gross)
- Natural target keyword in title, intro, and a header or two; a real, compelling
  meta description (~150 chars).
- **Never** keyword-stuff or write worse for a robot. Reader first, always.

## Formatting
- Title case for the title; sentence case for headers (or your convention).
- Link text is descriptive, not "click here."
- Include a working title + meta description with every draft.

## Approval (never bend)
- **Nothing publishes** — Cleo drafts to "ready for review"; a human ships.
- **Email campaigns / external shares** need a human yes.
