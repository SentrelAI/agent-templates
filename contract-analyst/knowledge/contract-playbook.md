## Standard positions (edit to yours — these are sane startup defaults)
- **Liability cap:** mutual, capped at 12 months' fees; cap must **apply to the
  indemnity** (watch the carve-out).
- **Indemnification:** mutual; each party for its own breach/negligence/IP
  infringement — never one-way against us, never uncapped.
- **IP:** each party keeps its pre-existing + independently developed IP; work
  product terms explicit; **no assignment of our IP**, ever, without a red flag.
- **Term + renewal:** prefer no auto-renewal; if auto, ≤12-month terms and a
  notice window ≥30 days that we track. No multi-year lock-ins without a
  price lock.
- **Termination:** for convenience for **both** parties (or neither), 30 days'
  notice; data return/deletion within 30 days; fees prorated on termination.
- **Payment:** Net 30; no unilateral price increases; renewals at then-current
  pricing = 🟡 flag (cap the increase).
- **Confidentiality:** mutual, 2-3 year tail; standard carve-outs.
- **Governing law:** [your state/jurisdiction]; arbitration acceptable; fee-
  shifting and jury waivers = 🟡.
- **Assignment:** mutual — if they can assign to an acquirer, so can we.
- **Incorporated ToS at a URL:** 🟡 minimum; 🔴 if they can amend unilaterally
  without notice.

## Hard lines (🔴 always — escalate to counsel before signature)
1. Uncapped liability or indemnity (or a cap the indemnity is carved out of).
2. Assignment of our IP, or IP reach into our pre-existing/independent work.
3. Exclusivity, non-compete, or customer non-solicit against us.
4. Unilateral right to change terms or pricing mid-term.
5. Auto-renewal locking >12 months with <30-day notice.
6. Personal guarantees; unusual warranties (uptime we can't meet, "defect-free").

## Escalation
- 🔴 → counsel + the deciding human, before signature, package prepared.
- Counterparty lawyer responds to a redline → counsel, always.
- Enforceability / jurisdiction / regulatory / dispute / possible breach →
  counsel. {{agent_name}} never fields these beyond analysis.

## The absolute rules
1. **No legal advice** — analysis against this playbook only; "counsel calls it."
2. **Nothing approved or signed** — no "fine to sign," ever.
3. **No negotiation with the counterparty** — suggestions go to our humans.
4. **Nothing sent to the other side** without a human's yes.
