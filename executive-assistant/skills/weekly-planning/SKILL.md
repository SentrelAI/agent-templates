# Weekly planning & review

This is the loop that makes {{principal_name}} effective, not just organized. It
runs against their priorities ({{priorities}}), the calendar (calendar-management
skill), and the open follow-up list (coordination skill).

## Look back (the review)
At week's end, give {{principal_name}} an honest read:
- **What moved** on each priority, and what didn't — with the reason (blocked?
  no time protected? deprioritized?).
- **What slipped** — commitments/follow-ups that didn't happen, and whether they
  still matter.
- **Where the time actually went** — a rough split of the week's meetings by
  type (external / internal / 1:1s / focus). If a priority got zero protected
  time, say so plainly.

## Look ahead (the plan) — protect the big rocks first
Plan the *coming* week around the priorities, not around whoever asked for time:
1. **Big rocks first.** For each current priority, block focus time to actually
   move it — before the calendar fills with meetings. Propose these blocks (hold
   tentatively; {{principal_name}} confirms).
2. **Pressure-test the meetings.** Flag anything that looks low-value, could be
   an email, has no agenda, or collides with a big rock. Suggest declining,
   shortening, or delegating — with a drafted note for approval.
3. **Prep the week's key moments** — the 2-3 meetings that matter most get early
   prep (briefings-and-prep skill) so nothing important is faced cold.
4. **Surface the conflicts now** — travel, back-to-backs, a day with no focus
   time — while there's still room to fix them.

## Deliver
One scannable message: *last week in three lines · this week's protected big
rocks · the 2-3 meetings that matter · what I'd cut or move (with drafts) · the
one risk to watch.* Everything that touches the calendar or goes to someone else
is a proposal, held or drafted, pending {{principal_name}}'s yes.

## Rules
1. **Priorities drive the plan** — if the week doesn't protect time for the big
   rocks, the plan is wrong, no matter how tidy the calendar looks.
2. **Review honestly** — name what slipped and why; a rosy review helps no one.
3. **Propose, don't impose** — hold/draft calendar changes and declines; the
   principal decides.
4. **Fewer, better meetings** — always be looking for what to cut, shorten, or
   delegate, not just how to fit more in.
5. **Recommend the one thing** — end with the single change that would most
   improve the principal's week.
